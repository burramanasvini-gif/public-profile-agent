# Profile: Satya Narayana Nadella

## Executive Summary

Satya Nadella is the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Microsoft, having succeeded Steve Ballmer as CEO in February 2014 and John W. Thompson as Chairman in June 2021. Born in Hyderabad, India, he is credited with steering Microsoft's transformation into a cloud- and AI-first company, overseeing acquisitions such as LinkedIn, GitHub, and Activision Blizzard and growing the company's market capitalization into the trillions of dollars. He is widely recognized for a leadership style centered on empathy and a 'growth mindset' culture.

## Biography / Summary

Satya Narayana Nadella was born on August 19, 1967, in Hyderabad, India, to a Telugu Hindu family. His father, Bukkapuram Nadella Yugandhar, was an Indian Administrative Service officer, and his mother, Prabhavati, was a Sanskrit lecturer. He attended Hyderabad Public School, Begumpet, and played cricket on his school team, a formative influence he has said shaped his approach to leadership and teamwork.

Nadella earned a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the Manipal Institute of Technology (Mangalore University) in 1988. He then moved to the United States, completing a master's degree in computer science at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee in 1990. He later earned an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business in 1997, and now serves on that university's board of trustees.

He began his career on the technology staff at Sun Microsystems before joining Microsoft in 1992, initially working on the Windows NT operating system. Over more than two decades at Microsoft, he held a series of increasingly senior roles, including vice president of the Microsoft bCentral small-business service, corporate vice president of Microsoft Business Solutions, senior vice president of R&D for the online services division, and president of Microsoft's Server and Tools business. Immediately before becoming CEO, he was executive vice president of Microsoft's Cloud and Enterprise group, where he led the build-out of the company's Azure cloud platform.

On February 4, 2014, Nadella was named Microsoft's third CEO, following co-founder Bill Gates and Steve Ballmer -- the first CEO in company history who was neither a founder nor an original employee. In 2021 he additionally became Chairman. Under his leadership, Microsoft pivoted from a Windows/Office-centric hardware and licensing model toward cloud computing and, more recently, artificial intelligence, including a major strategic alliance with OpenAI beginning in 2019. He led the company's largest acquisitions, including LinkedIn ($26.2 billion, 2016), GitHub ($7.5 billion, 2018), and Activision Blizzard ($69 billion, 2023). In 2017 he published a memoir, 'Hit Refresh', reflecting on his life, his son's disability, and his approach to reshaping Microsoft's culture around empathy and continuous learning.
